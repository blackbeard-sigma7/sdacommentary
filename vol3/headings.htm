WHITE COMMENTSPSALM 128ELLEN G. WHITE COMMENTSPSALM 129INTRODUCTION.—Ps. 129 is a song celebrating national deliverance. The psalmist speaks of the trials through which Israel as a nation had passed and of how the Lord interposed on their behalf and brought confusion to their enemies. It is a song of the countryside and has many allusions to agricultural pursuits. The precise historical incident that may have been the occasion of this psalm is a matter of conjecture. The psalmist is not so much concerned with the incident as with the lesson to be drawn from it.PSALM 130ELLEN G. WHITE COMMENTSPSALM 131ELLEN G. WHITE COMMENTSELLEN G. WHITE COMMENTSPSALM 133ELLEN G.
